Asynchronous Learning

Asynchronous learning, in contrast to synchronous learning, offers the flexibility to learn at your own pace and without the need for real-time interactions with an instructor.

Synchronous Learning

Synchronous learning refers to instructors and students gathering at the same time and (virtual or physical) place and interacting in “real-time”

MICRO LEARNING

Microlearning examples include short videos, interactive quizzes, infographics, podcasts, job aids, flashcards, simulations, social media posts, and gamified modules, all designed as brief, focused content (seconds to 15 mins) for immediate, on-demand learning

SOCIAL LEARNING

Social learning is a process by which people acquire knowledge and skills through social interactions. As opposed to traditional models of learning, it encourages learners to communicate with one another and share experiences in order to retain information.

COLLABORATIVE LEARNING

Collaborative learning is an educational approach where students work together in small groups to solve problems, complete tasks, or understand new concepts."

BLENDED LEARNING

Combines online digital learning with traditional face-to-face classroom instruction
